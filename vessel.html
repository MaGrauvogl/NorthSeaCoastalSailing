The appropriate Boat for the Wadden Sea

When I moved next to the North Sea from the South of Germany I started immediatly learning to sail. After a while I was looking for an affordable sailing vessel that was especially made for the Coastal Region of the North Sea: The Wadden Sea.

The most important is what happens during low tide when the water vanishes. At some areas the water depth may be very low and often lower than 2 meters. In some areas the water vanishes completely.
The most important feature of an boat appropiate for that region is the keel. The keel type determines the draft of the boat.

The most appropriate keel typs for the North Sea Coastal Region and the Wadden Sea are the bilge keel and the daggerboard.
Thus the vessel won't lay aside (hover on the picture right) when the water vanishes during low tide.

Our vessel to explore the German North Sea Coast

In the picture above you see our sailing vessel to explore the German North Sea Coast. It is a snapdragon  29 from the shipyard Thames Marine in England. Thames Marine build a series of family friendly sailing vessel from the years 1959 to 1981. Our Boat was built in 1971. The vessel has a bilge keele with a draft of only 1 meter.
The picture was made during low tide between the Coast and the East Fresien Islands on our way back home from the Island Spiekeroog. On low tide most of the area falls dry, that means all the water is vanished.
On the picture we are waiting for the flood to go on with our home bound journey
