A few generall informaton about the North Sea

North Sea seen by Satelite

Here you see a picture of the North Sea recorded by a NASA satelite.On the West you see Great Britain, in the South-East Denmark is visible and above Denmark there is Norway. In the South East you see Germany and going to the Wast the Netherland, Belgium and France appear.

Currents in the North Sea

The arrows in the picture show you the water current in the North Sea. Between high tide and low tide a huge amount of water has to flow from the Nord Sea in the Atlantic and vice versa. The speed of that current can reach 5kts and more. And this is a lot for small sailing vessel with a maximum speed of 6 to 8 kts.

Sandbanks at low tide

You see on the picture the sandbanks very typically for the coastal region of the north see. During low tide they are visible like on the picture. But at high tide the are right beneate the water surface and unvisible. Therefore they can be very dangerous for sailors in that area.

The Wadden Sea

You see a map of the so called Wadden Sea (the area inside the red lines). The Wadden Sea is a very special region on the North Sea Coast of the Netherlands, Germany and Denmark. During low tide this area falls almost completely dry. The Wadden Sea is a National Park and UNESCO World Heritage Center.

Walking on ground of Sea

During low tide most of the Wadden Sea falls dry. During that period you can walk on the ground of the Sea. With a local guide you can even walk to the island only a few miles off the coast.
